Temperature Facts

Babies have a higher body temperature than adults, typically around 37.5°C (99.5°F)

Shivering and Heat Production: When you're cold, shivering helps generate heat. It can increase your body temperature by about 2-5°C

Your hands and feet are more sensitive to temperature changes because they have fewer blood vessels, making them more prone to feeling cold.
